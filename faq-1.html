Common Questions About Elder Law & Estate Planning

1. What is Medicaid planning, and how can it protect my assets?

Medicaid planning is a crucial strategy designed to help seniors and their families qualify for Medicaid benefits while preserving as much of their assets as possible. Without proper planning, the high cost of long-term care can quickly deplete your savings and property. Our experienced attorneys at Scales Elder Law work closely with you to create personalized plans that comply with state and federal rules. We help you understand the complex eligibility requirements and implement legal tools such as asset transfers, trusts, and spend-down strategies to safeguard your financial future. Don’t risk losing your hard-earned assets—get expert guidance to protect your legacy.

2. How do I know if a loved one is a victim of nursing home abuse, and what can I do?

Recognizing nursing home abuse can be difficult, but it’s essential to act quickly to protect your loved one’s safety and dignity. Common signs include unexplained injuries, sudden behavioral changes, poor hygiene, or withdrawal. If you suspect abuse or neglect, contact our law firm immediately. We specialize in investigating these cases and pursuing strong legal action against responsible parties. Our compassionate team will guide you through the process of securing compensation and ensuring your loved one receives proper care. Don’t wait—your vigilance can make all the difference.

3. What are the key differences between wills, trusts, and probate?

Understanding wills, trusts, and probate is vital for effective estate planning. A will is a legal document that outlines how you want your assets distributed after death, but it typically requires the probate court’s supervision to validate. A trust is a more flexible estate planning tool that can help avoid probate, provide privacy, and manage assets during your lifetime and after. Probate is the court process that validates a will, settles debts, and distributes assets. At Scales Elder Law, we help you choose and implement the right combination of wills and trusts to minimize probate complexities, reduce taxes, and ensure your wishes are honored.
