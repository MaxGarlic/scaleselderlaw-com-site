Common Questions About Elder Law & Estate Planning

1. How can Medicaid planning protect my assets while qualifying for long-term care?

Medicaid planning is essential for seniors who want to preserve their hard-earned assets while qualifying for Medicaid assistance with nursing home or long-term care costs. At Scales Elder Law, we help you navigate the complex eligibility rules and strategically structure your finances to meet Medicaid requirements without depleting your savings. Our expert attorneys analyze your unique situation to create customized asset protection plans, including trusts and spend-down strategies, so you can secure the care you need while safeguarding your legacy for your loved ones.

2. What should I do if I suspect nursing home abuse or neglect?

If you suspect a loved one is experiencing nursing home abuse or neglect, immediate action is critical. Abuse can take many forms, including physical harm, emotional mistreatment, or financial exploitation. Our compassionate team at Scales Elder Law will guide you through the legal steps to protect your family member, investigate the situation thoroughly, and hold the responsible parties accountable. We work tirelessly to ensure justice and obtain compensation for pain and suffering, while also helping you explore safer care alternatives.

3. Why is having a will or trust important for estate planning?

Wills and trusts are fundamental tools in estate planning that provide peace of mind and control over how your assets are distributed after your passing. A well-drafted will ensures your wishes are honored, designates guardians for minor children, and simplifies probate proceedings. Trusts offer additional benefits such as avoiding probate, minimizing estate taxes, and protecting assets from creditors. At Scales Elder Law, we tailor wills and trusts to fit your family’s needs, ensuring your estate plan is comprehensive, up-to-date, and legally sound to protect your legacy for future generations.
